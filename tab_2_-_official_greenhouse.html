The VENUS FLYTRAP, also known as Dionaea muscipula, is a carnivorous plant located in wetlands on the East coast of the United States, such as in North and South Carolina. When the tiny hairs on the inside surface of the plants are touched by an insect, the plant will prepare to trap the insect by closing its leaves.


For more

information on

Venus Flytraps:

Venus Flytrap

Fun Facts  >>

Click here

  • The name "Venus Flytrap" comes from the Roman Godess of love, Venus.
  • The Venus Flytrap will only snap shut if its hairs are stimulated multiple times in about 20 seconds, to determine whether the prey has enough nutritional value to catch.
  • The Venus Flytrap is often cultivated for sale and is a vulnerable plant.

The COMMON FERN, also known as Tracheophyta, is an indoor or outdoor plant found around the globe. It characteristically has leaves, or fronds, that drape downwards. They are easy to grow and maintain, and make for wonderful indoor or garden decor.


For more

information on

the common fern:

Common Fern

Fun Facts  >>

Click here

  • There are more than 20,000 varieties of ferns around the world.
  • Ferns tend to grow long leaves that spread out in many directions.
  • Some outdoor ferns are built to grow in harsh weather. They can sometimes withstand up to temperatures of -30 degrees Celsius!
  • Ferns are currently not endangered.

The DANDELION, also known as Taraxacum, is a perennial herb commonly found across the globe. The dandelion flower blooms yellow before opening up and revealing its many seeds attached to fluffy white "pappus". The seeds are then spread and planted by wind, without the need for pollination.


For more

information on

the dandelion:

Dandelions

Fun Facts  >>

Click here

  • Dandelion can be used in herbal teas and medical treatment.
  • Dandelions are excellent sources of vitamins A, C, and K.
  • The yellow-pigmented flowers are often used as yellow dye for fabric and other products.
  • Dandelions are currently not endangered.

The SUCCULENT, also known as Xerophyte, is a common plant that has adapted to living in dry environments due to its ability to store water. It is mostly found in climates with high temperatures and low rainfall, such as deserts, but can also be an indoor potted plant suitable for decor. Aloe Vera and Cacti are types of succulents. Purchase or collect them from the plantinator and watch them grow!

Succulent

Fun Facts  >>

  • The succulent plant's name comes from the Latin word sucus, meaning juice or sap.
  • Due to the nature of succulents, they are resistant to droughts.
  • Most succulents are currently not endangered; however, certain species of succulents are threatened with extinction due to poaching.

The CACTUS, also known as Cactaceae, is a spiny plant found in extremely dry and hot environments, and comes in many shapes and sizes. Cacti are a type of succulent; their thick stems allow them to store a lot of water. They have ribs that allow them to quickly absorb water during and after rainfall. Smaller species of cacti can be grown indoors.


For more

information on

the cactus:

Cactus

Fun Facts  >>

Click here

  • Cactus spines are made from aeroles, structures which produce the cactus' spines and flowers.
  • Cacti have short growing seasons and their protective stands are easily able to withstand drought.
  • Cacti spines are spiky and will hurt if they are touched.
  • Cacti are mostly not endangered; however, some species are subject to illegal trade and are threatened.

The ALOE VERA is a type of succulent that grows in the wild in hot, dry climates. Its stems store water, and the spikes on the plant prevent water loss. It is known to have skin healing properties and is usually applied directly to the skin through lotion or consumed orally. They can also make for excellent household plants.


Aloe Vera

Fun Facts  >>

  • Aloe Vera's leaves typically grow straight up.
  • Because of its useful medicinal properties, Aloe Vera is cultivated in tropical regions such as Arizona.
  • Even though Aloe Vera has previously shown health benefits, it is best to consult your doctor before using any Aloe Vera products.
  • Aloe Vera is currently not endangered.

The ZEBRA PLANT, also known as Aphelandra squarrosa, is a type of plant native to the Atlantic Forest in Brazil. However, it is now commonly seen and used as a householdplant. Its shape and structure is somewhat similar to that of Aloe Vera, and does not bloom often. Its dark leaves are lined with white lines, and its color ressembles that of a zebra.

The ROSE, also known as Rosa, is a plant well known for its exuberant flower petals and its sweet fragrance. Roses have thorns on their stems in order to prevent species who are attracted to their sweet fragrances from consuming them. Differently colored roses are known to symbolize different things, such as love or friendship. There are also hybrid roses which consist of the genetic

modification of rose seeds to create

new roses with multiple colors.

The CHINESE MONEY PLANT, also known as Pilea peperomioides, coin plant, pancake plant, and UFO plant is an easy to maintain household plant. Its characteristic coin shaped leaves are what gives it the popular money plant name. It originates from the Cang mountain range in Yunnan, China, and can only be found in China. It is best grown in temperate climates, making it the perfect household plant.


For more information on

Chinese money plants:


Chinese Money Plant

Fun Facts  >>

Click here

  • The Chinese Money Plant was originally believed to bring good luck, money and fortune to its owner
  • Because the large, circular leaves stack on top of each other, they look like coins.
  • It makes a wonderful windowsill plant that performs well in dry conditions and low light.
  • The Chinese Money Plant is currently endangered in its native region.
